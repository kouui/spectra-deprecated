src.Atomic.LTELib.EinsteinA_to_EinsteinBs_hz¶

src.Atomic.LTELib.EinsteinA_to_EinsteinBs_hz(Aji, f0, gi, gj)[source]¶

given Einstein A coefficient Aij, calculate Einstein B coefficients Bij and Bji.

Parameters

  • Aji (np.double or array-like) – Einstein A coefficient Aji, [\(s^{-1}\)]

  • f0 (np.double or array-like) – central frequency of corresponding line transition, [\(Hz\)]

  • gi (np.uint8 or array-like) – statistical weight of lower level, [-]

  • gj (np.uint8 or array-like) – statistical weight of upper level, [-]

Returns

  • Bji (np.double or array-like) – Einstein B coefficient Bji, [\(s^{-1}/(erg/cm^{2}/Sr/Hz/s)\)]

  • Bij (np.double or array-like) – Einstein B coefficient Bji, [\(s^{-1}/(erg/cm^{2}/Sr/Hz/s)\)]

Notes

Einstein Relations for bound-bound transitions 1.

\[B_{ji} = A_{ji} / \frac{2h\nu^{3}}{c^{2}}\]
\[B_{ij} = B_{ji} \frac{g_{j}}{g_{i}}\]

References

1

Ivan Hubeny, Dimitri Mihalas, “Theory of Stellar Atmosphere: An Introduction to Astrophysical Non-equilibrium Quantitative Spectroscopic Analysis”, Princeton University Press, pp. 117, 2015.
