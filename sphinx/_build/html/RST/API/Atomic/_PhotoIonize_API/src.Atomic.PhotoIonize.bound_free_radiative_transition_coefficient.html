src.Atomic.PhotoIonize.bound_free_radiative_transition_coefficient¶

src.Atomic.PhotoIonize.bound_free_radiative_transition_coefficient(wave, J, alpha, Te, nk_by_ni_LTE)[source]¶

Given wavelength mesh, mean intensity (as function of wavelength), photoionization cross section, compute

  • radiative ionization rate,

  • stimulated radiative recombination rate,

  • spontaneous radiative recombination rate.

Parameters

  • wave (array-like of np.double) – wavelength mesh, [\(cm\)]

  • J (array-like of np.double) – mean intensity as function of wavelength, [\(erg/cm^2/Sr/cm/s\)]

  • alpha (array-like of np.double) – photoionization cross section as function of wavelength, [\(cm^{2}\)]

  • Te (np.double) – Temperature, [\(K\)]

  • nk_by_ni_LTE (np.double) – population ratio in LTE, nk/ni

Returns

  • Rik (np.double) – Radiative ionization rate, [\(s^{-1} \cdot cm^{-3}\)]

  • Rki_stim (np.double) – Stimulated Radiative ionization rate, [\(s^{-1} \cdot cm^{-3}\)]

  • Rki_spon (np.double) – Spontaneous Radiative ionization rate, [\(s^{-1} \cdot cm^{-3}\)]

Notes

Radiative ionization rate 1 Equation(9.43),

\[R_{ik} = 4\pi \int_{\nu_0}^{\infty} \alpha_{ik}(\nu) (h\nu)^{-1} J(\nu) d\nu\]

Stimulated radiative recombination rate 1 Equation(9.47),

\[R_{ki}^{stim} = \frac{n_i^{LTE}}{n_k^{LTE}} 4\pi \int_{\nu_0}^{\infty} \alpha_{ik}(\nu) (h\nu)^{-1} J(\nu) e^{-h\nu/kT} d\nu\]

Spontaneous radiative recombination rate 1 Equation(9.45)

\[R_{ki}^{stim} = \frac{n_i^{LTE}}{n_k^{LTE}} 4\pi \int_{\nu_0}^{\infty} \alpha_{ik}(\nu) (h\nu)^{-1} (2h\nu^{3}/c^2) e^{-h\nu/kT} d\nu\]

References

1(1,2,3)

Ivan Hubeny, Dimitri Mihalas, “Theory of Stellar Atmosphere: An Introduction to Astrophysical Non-equilibrium Quantitative Spectroscopic Analysis”, Princeton University Press, pp. 273, 2015.
